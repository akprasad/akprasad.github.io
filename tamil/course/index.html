Introduction

Tamil is spoken by more than 80 million people worldwide, primarily in its ancestral homelands of southern India and Sri Lanka. Its rich history traces back thousands of years, when the Pandyan kings roamed in their chariots over the lands of Tamil Nadu.

The Tamil world uses a common standard for writing and other formal occasions. This standard, which we can call formal Tamil, is based on how Tamil was used seven hundred years ago. But seven hundred years is a very long time. Today, each community has its own informal Tamil with its own sounds, its own phrases, and its own charm. While formal Tamil is the language of literature and high culture, informal Tamil is the language of home and of everyday life.

This course focuses on informal Tamil, specifically the consensus style heard in films and other popular media. Our approach is simple: constant exposure to interesting language. This means a steady volume of rich content that is not too hard and not too easy. This is the key to unlock your abilities and make real progress that lasts.

But how? Through our library of colorful stories made specifically for Tamil learners. Our stories start out with simple language that even complete beginners can read and enjoy. Little by little, they grow more complex, until they are hardly different from the natural Tamil that courses through the world.

We hope that these stories will help you uncover the rich beauty of Tamil.
